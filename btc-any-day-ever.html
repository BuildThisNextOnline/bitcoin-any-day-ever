But price is the wrong thing to watch.
The right question is: "across all of history, what has actually been the probability of coming out ahead?"

What this shows:
For each holding period, the chart answers: if you bought on any random day in Bitcoin's history and held for exactly this long, what fraction of the time would you have been in profit?

Short-term is noisy — closer to a coin flip. But something shifts around the two-year mark. By three years, losing money becomes historically rare.
The behavioural gap

Most retail investors don't lose money because Bitcoin is a bad asset.

They lose money because they sell during drawdowns — converting a temporary paper loss into a permanent real one.

The exit made it permanent.

Each cell is a unique buy/sell combination — a week you could have bought paired with a later week you could have sold. Green means profit. Red means loss.

How to read this:
X-axis (bottom) = buy date, 2010 at bottom-left, 2026 at bottom-right
Y-axis (right) = sell date, 2010 at bottom-right, 2026 at top-right
The diagonal runs bottom-left → top-right — buy and sell on the same week.
Above the diagonal is the valid data.
Below is blank — you cannot sell before you buy.
Hover any cell to inspect.
A note on the data: ~780 weekly intervals, each using the average closing price across all 7 days in that week. The probability curve in tab 01 uses the full daily dataset.

Bitcoin has crashed 80% or more — multiple times.
Every cycle produced a new wave of people who bought near the top, watched it fall, and sold at the bottom.

The data shows this was almost never a problem with Bitcoin.
It was a problem with time horizon.
The investors who got hurt were not unlucky.
They were impatient.

Stop asking "what is the price today?"
Start asking "what is the probability of being right over my actual time horizon?"

The first produces anxiety.
The second produces a framework.

And that framework leads to the only question that actually matters:
"What is the right time horizon for me to have to ensure that my Bitcoin holdings are going to be profitable?"
